The Artist

Katherine Houston

A Houston native whose practice spans over two decades, Katherine Houston works in a medium uniquely her own. She paints in reverse — applying acrylic to the back of clear Plexiglas, building luminous abstract worlds where each brushstroke is a commitment, unalterable.

Her work explores what she calls "the surprising outcome of letting go" — compositions that begin with color and expand into landscape, cityscape, and pure abstraction, all seen through a surface that seems to generate its own light.

Education Glassell School of Art · Univ. of Houston · Hudson River Valley
Galleries Laura Rathe Fine Art · The Russell Collection · Jones & Terwilliger

The Process

Painting in Reverse

Studio Process Film

What makes a Houston painting immediately recognizable is a depth impossible to achieve on canvas. She works on the back of transparent Plexiglas — the painting builds in reverse order.

The first mark made is the last thing visible. No corrections. Pure intention at every stroke.

1
Color Concept
Every work begins with a color palette — intuited from city, coast, or landscape.
2
Reverse Application
Acrylic is applied to the back of Plexiglas. Foreground details painted first; background last.
3
Inner Luminosity
Light passes through the Plexiglas and refracts through layers — creating the signature glow.
