About the Series

Painting,
Made Sculptural

The Cube Series extends Katherine's signature reverse-painting technique into three dimensions. Each cube is built from hand-cut Plexiglas panels that she paints from the back — the same approach she uses for her large-format paintings — then assembled into a precise six-sided form.

The result is a sculpture with an inner luminosity you cannot achieve on canvas. Light enters through the transparent acrylic and bounces between the painted surfaces, giving each cube a glow that shifts as you move around it. Displayed individually on a pedestal, arranged in clusters on a shelf, or mounted flush to a wall, they transform any space they occupy.

No two cubes are identical. Each face is painted independently, making every cube a genuinely unique object — six abstract paintings assembled into a single three-dimensional work.

The Process

Reverse-painted Plexiglas,
assembled by hand

01

Painting the faces

Each face begins as a clear Plexiglas panel. Katherine paints on the back surface using acrylics — the same technique she uses for her large-format paintings. The first mark becomes the outermost visible layer.

02

Curing and assembly

After each panel cures, she assembles all six into a precise cube form. The edges are hand-cut and joined with optical-clarity adhesive, leaving no visible seam.

03

The living object

Because the Plexiglas is optically clear, ambient light enters the cube and interacts with the painted surfaces. The result is a work that looks fundamentally different at dawn, midday, dusk, and under artificial light.
